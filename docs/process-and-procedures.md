## 3. Violation Notices

A proper violation notice should:

- Cite the **exact CC&R or rule allegedly violated**  
- Describe the **facts clearly** (what, where, when)  
- State your **right to appeal / request a hearing**  
- Give you a **clear timeframe** to respond

When notices:

- Omit the appeal right  
- Misdescribe the location or nature of the issue  
- Cite inapplicable CC&R sections  

â€¦they are **procedurally defective** and may not be enforceable as written.

## 4. Appeals & Hearings

Appeal hearings are serious. Think of them as:

> A quasi-judicial process where anything you say can be used against you later.

### What Should Happen

- You receive a written notice explaining your right to a hearing.  
- You request the hearing **in writing**, within the stated window.  
- A hearing is scheduled **within the timelines in the Bylaws**.  
- You get a fair chance to present your position.  
- The Board issues a decision in writing.

### What Has Sometimes Happened Instead

- Hearings scheduled **outside** the required timeline.  
- No appeal rights mentioned in initial notices.  
- New CC&R sections introduced for the first time at the hearing.  
- Management or Board members **changing their recollection** of prior conversations.  
- Heavy reliance on a catch-all â€œdiscretionâ€ clause (like Â§6.6) to justify a decision.
